The Challenge

Nexus Tech, a B2B SaaS startup in the project management space, launched with no brand identity — just a product name. They needed a complete visual identity system that could stand beside enterprise competitors like Asana and Monday.com, while feeling approachable to SMBs. The existing "logo" was a stock icon from Google Slides.

The Approach

I started with a 2-hour discovery call to extract brand personality keywords: "intelligent, precise, connected, human." I researched 20+ competitors and identified whitespace in the market — most B2B SaaS brands overindex on blue/gray corporate tones. We chose a bold red-orange gradient on a dark base to signal confidence and energy without screaming "startup."

Deliverables

Primary logo (icon + wordmark), secondary horizontal variant, monochrome versions, favicon, color palette (6 colors with accessibility-checked contrasts), type system (2 fonts), brand guidelines PDF (48 pages), stationery set, and social media avatar kit.

Design Process
1
Discovery & Brief

Brand questionnaire + 1hr call to define positioning, competitors, and visual direction.

2
Moodboard & Direction

3 distinct moodboards presented for client vote before any logo sketching began.

3
Concept Exploration

20+ logo sketches refined to 3 polished concepts presented in Figma with context mockups.

4
Refinement + System Build

Selected concept refined, full brand system built, guidelines documented.

5
Final Delivery

AI, SVG, PNG, PDF files + Figma source + full brand guide delivered via Google Drive.

Outcome

Client used the brand identity in a Seed funding pitch deck. Investors specifically commented on the "polished, enterprise-ready branding." Nexus Tech raised $280K in their first round. Brand recall in user surveys improved 40% vs their pre-rebrand placeholder identity.

The Problem

FinFlow's analytics dashboard had a 68% drop-off in onboarding and a Net Promoter Score of 12. User interviews revealed the interface was overwhelming — critical KPIs were buried, the navigation made users feel "lost," and mobile experience was completely broken. Customer support was spending 40% of tickets on "how do I find X" questions.

Research Methods

6 user interviews with current FinFlow customers, 2 competitive benchmarking sessions (Mixpanel, Amplitude), task analysis of 5 core user flows, heatmap review of existing dashboard using Hotjar data, and creation of 3 user personas covering the primary audience segments (Growth Marketer, Product Manager, CEO).

Key Design Decisions

Adopted a progressive disclosure pattern — showing summary KPIs first, with drill-down on demand. Rebuilt navigation around jobs-to-be-done rather than feature categories. Introduced a "Quick Insights" panel surfacing AI-generated anomalies. Rebuilt the mobile layout from scratch using a tab-based bottom navigation.

Design Process
1
User Research

Interviews, persona creation, journey mapping, and heuristic audit of existing product.

2
Information Architecture

Card sorting exercise with 8 users. Rebuilt IA from scratch. Navigation reduced from 14 items to 6.

3
Wireframes

Low-fidelity wireframes for all 40+ screens, tested with 3 users before proceeding to high-fidelity.

4
High-Fidelity UI + Design System

Built a full Figma component library with 120+ components, tokens, and documentation.

5
Usability Testing + Handoff

Moderated usability test with 5 participants. Iterated on 8 pain points found. Clean Figma handoff with dev notes.

Outcome

After launch, task success rate increased from 38% to 62%. Monthly churn dropped from 8.4% to 5.3%. NPS improved from 12 to 41. Support tickets related to navigation dropped 72% in 30 days. FinFlow's CEO quoted: "The redesign literally saved our retention numbers."

The Goal

Conversion Strategy

1
Conversion Audit

2
Wireframe + Copy Structure

4
Webflow Development Handoff

Delivered Figma files with developer annotations. Supported developer during Webflow build with 2 revision calls.

Outcome

Conversion rate increased from 1.8% to 2.5% (Variant A) and 2.9% (Variant B — winner). Bounce rate decreased 22%. GrowthLeap's CAC from paid ads dropped $18 per acquisition due to improved conversion efficiency. The page has been live for 8 months with no redesign needed.

The Brief

A product agency building 6 SaaS tools needed a unified icon system to create visual consistency across all products. Existing icons were pulled from 3 different free libraries (Feather, Heroicons, FontAwesome), creating a mismatched, unprofessional feel. They needed a custom set covering navigation, actions, status indicators, and domain-specific icons for their CRM, analytics, and HR tools.

Design System Approach

Established a strict 24×24 grid with 2px stroke width for "outline" style, filled variant, and a duotone version using brand secondary color. Every icon: consistent terminal cap (round), corner radius (2px), optical balance adjustments on geometric shapes. Delivered as individual SVGs + Figma component library with variants and auto-sizing.

Design Process
1
Icon Audit + Category Mapping

Catalogued 200+ icons needed across 6 products. Deduplicated to 120 unique icons in 10 categories.

2
Style Definition

Designed 10 sample icons in 3 style directions. Client chose "rounded stroke" direction.

3
Production

Designed all 120 icons (360 variants including outline/fill/duotone) in Illustrator on a 24px grid.

4
Figma Library Build

Imported all SVGs into Figma. Built component library with variant switching and size properties.

Outcome

Icon system adopted across all 6 agency products. Developer team reported 4 hours/week saved on icon-hunting. The Figma library became the "single source of truth" for all design/dev handoffs. Agency rated the project 5/5 and commissioned a second set of 60 additional icons.
